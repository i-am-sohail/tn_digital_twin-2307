🎯 What is a Digital Twin?

A digital twin is a real-time, data-driven virtual representation of a physical place — in this case, a Tamil Nadu district. This dashboard combines official district statistics with live environmental data and an AI reasoning engine to turn real numbers into actionable insights for planning and policy.

This project links district development data to the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) — a shared global call to end poverty, protect the planet, and ensure peace and prosperity by 2030. Tamil Nadu's digital twin helps identify which SDGs matter most for each district and how progress can be tracked.

This is a school AI project, built to explore how real data + AI can help with public planning. The AI insights are decision-support tools: they reason from real data but aren't official government guidance.

Population, area, and literacy figures come from Government of Tamil Nadu district data and Census 2011 (India's most recent literacy census). Weather comes live from OpenWeatherMap. Air quality comes live from WAQI, which aggregates real CPCB monitoring stations. Full technical details are in this project's API_INTEGRATION.md file.
